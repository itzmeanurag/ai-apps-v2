ACME CORPORATION – EMPLOYEE POLICY HANDBOOK
Version 3.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025
============================================================

1. PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
----------------------
Full-time employees accrue PTO at the following rates:
  - Years 0–2:   15 days per year (1.25 days/month)
  - Years 3–5:   20 days per year (1.67 days/month)
  - Years 6+:    25 days per year (2.08 days/month)

Part-time employees (20–39 hrs/week) accrue PTO at 50% of the full-time rate.
PTO must be approved by your direct manager at least 5 business days in advance
for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

Maximum PTO carryover: 10 days into the next calendar year. Unused PTO above
this limit is forfeited on December 31.

2. SICK LEAVE
-------------
All employees receive 10 sick days per calendar year. Sick days do not carry
over and are not paid out upon termination. A doctor's note is required for
absences exceeding 3 consecutive sick days.

3. REMOTE WORK POLICY
---------------------
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval.
Remote work arrangements must be documented in the HR system. Employees are
expected to be available during core hours: 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM local time.
A reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps) is required for remote work.

4. CODE OF CONDUCT
------------------
All employees must:
  a) Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and professionalism.
  b) Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information.
  c) Report conflicts of interest to HR within 30 days of discovery.
  d) Complete annual compliance training by March 31 each year.
  e) Not engage in harassment, discrimination, or retaliation of any kind.

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and
including termination.

5. BENEFITS
-----------
Health Insurance:
  - Medical: Company covers 80% of premium for employee; 60% for dependents.
  - Dental: Company covers 70% of premium.
  - Vision: Company covers 60% of premium.
  Enrollment window: 30 days from hire date or during annual open enrollment
  (November 1–30).

Retirement:
  - 401(k) plan with company match of 4% of salary (100% match on first 4%).
  - Vesting schedule: 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years.

Professional Development:
  - Annual learning budget: $2,000 per employee.
  - Eligible expenses: courses, certifications, conferences, books.
  - Reimbursement requires manager approval and receipt submission within 60 days.

6. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
----------------------
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year:
  - Mid-year review: July (feedback only, no rating)
  - Annual review: December (rating + compensation adjustment)

Ratings scale: 1 (Needs Improvement) – 5 (Exceptional)
Employees rated 4 or 5 are eligible for merit increases of 3–8%.

7. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
-----------------------
Step 1: Verbal warning (documented in HR system)
Step 2: Written warning
Step 3: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) – 30 to 90 days
Step 4: Termination

Serious violations (theft, harassment, safety violations) may result in
immediate termination without prior steps.

8. GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
----------------------
Employees with workplace concerns should:
  1. Discuss with direct manager (if appropriate).
  2. Contact HR at hr@acmecorp.example.com.
  3. Submit a formal grievance via the HR portal.
  4. Request escalation to the Employee Relations team if unresolved within 10 days.

All grievances are treated confidentially to the extent possible.

9. EQUIPMENT AND TECHNOLOGY
---------------------------
Company-issued equipment must be used primarily for business purposes.
Personal use is permitted within reasonable limits. Employees are responsible
for the security of company devices. Lost or stolen devices must be reported
to IT Security within 4 hours of discovery.

Software installation on company devices requires IT approval.

10. TRAVEL POLICY
-----------------
Business travel must be pre-approved by the department head.
Reimbursable expenses:
  - Flights: Economy class for trips under 6 hours; business class permitted
    for longer flights with VP approval.
  - Hotels: Up to $250/night in major cities; $175/night elsewhere.
  - Meals: Up to $75/day (receipts required for amounts over $25).
  - Ground transportation: Actual cost (Uber/Lyft preferred over taxis).

Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of travel completion.
